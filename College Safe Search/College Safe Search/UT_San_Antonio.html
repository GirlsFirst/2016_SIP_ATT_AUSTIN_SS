    • Policies

    Scope: UTSA policy applies to University administrators, faculty, staff, students, visitors, and applicants.

    Definitions: Consent- voluntary, mutually understandable agreement. Consent is not implied even within a relationship, must clearly display willingness. Consent doesn’t apply if there was physical force, threats, intimidation, coercion, or incapacitation. Incapacitation- state of being prevents ability of someone to give consent eg. under influence of drugs, alcohol, or one individual being asleep or unconscious,etc. Title IX Coordinator- senior University administrator who ensures University’s compliance with Title IX. Title IX Coordinator leads the administrative investigation of reported Sexual Misconduct, and gives assistance, support, and education to the victim.

    School Responsibilities: After an alleged rape has been reported the school Equal Opportunity Service Office, Title IX Coordinator, UTSA Police Department, and Student Counseling Services will work together to investigate and resolve the complaint. They will ask the alleged victim how they want to participate in the investigation, and also advise the alleged victim on filing a criminal report.

    Investigation: During the investigation both the alleged victim and the alleged rapist can submit relevant evidence. Witnesses and other person who may have relevant information will be interviewed. After the investigation is complete the proper administration will be informed of hte issue, and then the administration will take disciplinary action.
