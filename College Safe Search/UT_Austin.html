Policies

Scope: UT Policies apply to all students, employees,visitors, and applicants. If the incident occurs on campus, at an official UT function or student organization event, or occurs in a location with connection to UT,UT will conduct on investigation.

Investigation: Once the report was filed the University will respond promptly and open an investigation. The investigation will include interviewing the parties and witnesses to gather and asses evidence.

Definition of Consent: Consent is voluntary, mutually understandable agreement that clearly indicates a willingness to engage in each instance of sexual activity. Consent can not be a result of physical force, threat, coercion, incapacitation, or any other factor that would make a person incapable of exercise their free will to choose.

Definition of Incapacitation: A state of being that prevents a person for being able to give consent; drugs or alcohol, unconsciousness or being asleep, or because of another disability could make someone incapacitated.

Support: The student can go to the Office of the Dean of Students, Student Emergency Services for information about assistancd with class, living arrangements, or University-provided support or referalls needed as a result of the allegation.

If the University failed to apply with any issue layed out in this policies contacy the University Title IX Coordinator titleix@austin.utexas.edu
