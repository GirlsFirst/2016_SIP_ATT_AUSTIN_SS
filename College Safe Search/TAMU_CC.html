Policies

In 2014 TAMU-CC changed their policy to break sexual assault into 4 subcategories; rape, fondling,incest, statutory.

Definitions:Rape- penetration of the vagina or anus with any body part or object, or oral penetration by a sex organ of another person, without the consent of the victim.

Fondling- touching private parts of another person for thepurposes of sexual gratification, without the consent of the victim, including instances where the victim is incapable of giving consent because of his/her age or because ofhis/her temporary or permanent mental incapacity

Is considered sexual assault if consent was given because of the use of physical force or violence,threats, or intentionally impairing the other person by administering any substance without the other person’s knowledge."

Report sexual assault to the TAMU-CC Police Department and Mr. Rodriguez, the Title IX Coordinator (361) 825-2765.Once a sexual assault is reported TAMU-CC will tell your rights for charging sexual assault crimes and help you find counseling,mental health,and sexual assault advocacy.

If needed the university will provide medical help, housing changes, change in class schedules, and “no contact” from you and the accused party.TAMU-CC will help you file a protective Order.
