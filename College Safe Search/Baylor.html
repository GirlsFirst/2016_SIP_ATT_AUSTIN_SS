Policies

According to the University, “consensual sexual activity happens when each partner willingly and affirmatively chooses to participate.” A person incapacitated (by drugs, alcohol, or otherwise rendered mentally or physically helpless) is unable to consent. Baylor University prohibits all forms of sex discrimination, sexual violence (including all non-consensual sexual contact), and sexual harassment (including relationship violence, stalking, sexual exploitation, and retaliation). University employees who are allowed to information confidential are licensed professional mental health counselors, licensed medical professionals, and the Baylor University Chaplain. The University recommends that the survivor files a complaint with the Title IX Coordinator. Learn more.
